Taste and Traditions of Nueva Vizcaya

From sweet rice delicacies to indigenous festivals - explore the flavors and culture that make Vizcaya unqiue.

Local Delicacies

Each dish in Nueva Vizcaya tells a story - rooted in heritage, community, and celebration

Tupig

A beloved local delicacy made of glutinous rice and coconut, grilled in banana leaves for a smoky, sweet flavor

Pancit Cabagan

Though originating from Isabela, this flavorful noodle dish has become a local favorite across Nueva Vizcaya.

Ipon Fish

A rare seasonal catch from Vizcaya's rivers - lightly fried or cooked in vinegar for its unique taste and texture.

Festivals & Traditions

Vibrant colors, rhythmic dances, and heartfelt celebrations showcase the spirit of the Vizcayan people.

Panagyaman Festival

A province-wide Thanksgiving celebration marking the end of the harvest season — filled with parades and dances.

Ikat Weaving

A traditional weaving technique of the indigenous communities — known for its intricate patterns and craftsmanship.

Ammungan Festival

An annual celebration in Nueva Vizcaya, Philippines, that commemorates the province's founding anniversary and gathers its diverse indigenous and migrant communities

Heritage & Community

Beyond food and festivals, Nueva Vizcaya's greatest treasure is its people — proud, warm, and deeply connected to their roots.

Local Markets

Experience the province's bustling markets filled with fresh produce, handmade crafts, and local delicacies.

Vizcayan Hospitality

Meet the people of Nueva Vizcaya — known for their friendliness, humility, and warm sense of community.

Handicrafts & Local Art

From wood carvings to woven goods, local artisans preserve and share their culture through creative expression.
